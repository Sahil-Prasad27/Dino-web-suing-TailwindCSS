Dinosaur

Dinosaurs are a diverse group of reptiles from the clade Dinosauria, first appearing about 230 million years ago. Dominating terrestrial ecosystems after the Triassicâ€“Jurassic extinction event, they varied in size and form, from small, bird-like species to massive, long-necked giants. Dinosaurs are categorized into two main groups based on hip structure: Saurischia (lizard-hipped), including theropods like Tyrannosaurus rex and sauropodomorphs like Brachiosaurus, and Ornithischia (bird-hipped), including herbivores like Stegosaurus and Triceratops. Extinction events 66 million years ago largely wiped them out, except for the lineage that led to modern birds, their living descendants.

Herbivore with distinctive back plates.

Velociraptor

Long-necked herbivore, very tall stature.

Large predator with a sail-like spine.

Pteranodon

Flying reptile with a large wingspan.

Allosaurus

Large carnivorous dinosaur, fierce hunter.
